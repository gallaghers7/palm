The majority of hardware wallets are now supported by Palm, including all Ledger models and Trezor T. Do not transfer to Trezor One or other hardware wallets as they are not supported by Palm at this time. We are working with the manufacturers to resolve this.
Do not attempt to transfer funds directly to an exchange wallet on Palm. In order to move funds from Palm to an exchange such as Coinbase or Binance, you must first bridge the funds back to Ethereum mainnet. Not doing so can result in your funds being irretrievable.
Transactions may take hours to complete. This is not uncommon, particularly during periods of high traffic on Ethereum mainnet. Please be patient, and rest assured that no assets have ever been lost while bridging between Palm and mainnet.

Download and install the MetaMask wallet

If you’re looking to purchase an NFT or transact in cryptocurrency on Palm, you’ll need a wallet of some kind. This site currently supports the commonly-used MetaMask wallet. This is installed as a browser extension, with support for Chrome, Firefox, Brave & Edge.
Get MetaMask

Configure your MetaMask wallet

Out-of-the box, MetaMask only supports Ethereum networks, so needs configuring to allow it to work on Palm. We make this process easy, and you can switch to the Palm network using the Configure MetaMask function below. This also adds the most commonly-used tokens on Palm to your wallet, and details for manual configuration are provided.

Configure MetaMask


Add the Palm network and its supported tokens to your MetaMask wallet.

Get assets on palm

DAI is a cryptocurrency pegged to the US Dollar, and is the most common token used to pay for NFTs and services on Palm. You can buy DAI tokens directly with your credit card using the fiat onramp service, or you can bring your existing DAI tokens from the Ethereum network to Palm using the token bridge. Using either method will reward you with PALM tokens which are used to pay for gas on Palm.
